Early Life

William Shakespeare was born in Stratford-upon-Avon in Warwickshire England, on or about April 23rd 1564. He was the 3rd of 8 children, his father John Shakespeare was a glove maker, and his mother Mary Arden came from a wealthy family. Shakespeare attended King’s New School, studying Latin and classics. At 18, he married Anne Hathaway and had three children, Susanna, and twins Hamnet and Judith. The loss of Hamnet at age 11 profoundly influenced his later writing.

London Years

Shakespeare moved to London in the 1580s/1590s, joined the Lord Chamberlain’s Men, and performed at the Globe Theatre. He wrote plays like Henry VI, Richard III, and co-founded the Globe in 1599. His comedies include A Midsummer Night’s Dream, and tragedies include Romeo and Juliet and Julius Caesar. He also invested in real estate and became a shareholder in the King's Men after James I ascended.

Later Works

Shakespeare wrote 39 plays, 154 sonnets, and long poems. His tragedies explored ambition, jealousy, and fate (Hamlet, Othello, Macbeth). Comedies explored love and humor (Twelfth Night, As You Like It). Historical plays dramatized past kings’ lives (Richard III, Henry V). His sonnets explored beauty, time, mortality, and love, shaping literature worldwide.

Legacy

Shakespeare’s legacy spans literature, drama, film, and language. He popularized over 1,000 words/phrases like "wild-goose chase" and "heart of gold." His characters are complex, emotionally realistic, and his plays set frameworks for storytelling. The Globe Theatre symbolizes world theatre, and Shakespeare continues to inspire artists globally.
