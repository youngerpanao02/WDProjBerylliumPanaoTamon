Shakespeare’s Timeless Words

Explore the wisdom, philosophy, and beauty of Shakespeare’s writing through these memorable quotes.

“To be, or not to be — that is the question.”
Hamlet1603
Hamlet reflects on the nature of existence, questioning whether it is nobler to endure suffering or to end one’s life.
“All the world’s a stage.”
As You Like It1599
Jacques compares human life to a theatrical performance in which individuals play different roles throughout their lives.
“Love looks not with the eyes.”
Midsummer1595
Helena explains that love is driven by emotion rather than logic or physical appearance.
“Some are born great.”
Twelfth Night1601
Greatness can come through birth, effort, or circumstance.
“We are such stuff as dreams are made on.”
The Tempest1611
This line explores identity, ambition, and the unpredictable nature of human experience.
“This above all: to thine own self be true.”
Hamlet1603
Polonius advises honesty and self-awareness, highlighting integrity as central to human behavior.
“Brevity is the soul of wit.”
Hamlet1603
Concise speech reflects wisdom, emphasizing Shakespeare’s use of irony and commentary on human nature.
“The fault, dear Brutus, is not in our stars.”
Julius Caesar1599
Cassius argues that people shape their own destinies rather than being controlled by fate.
“Cowards die many times before their deaths.”
Julius Caesar1599
Caesar suggests that fear causes repeated suffering, while bravery allows one to face death only once.
“If music be the food of love, play on.”
Twelfth Night1601
Duke Orsino opens the play by associating music with emotional intensity.
“What’s done cannot be undone.”
Macbeth1606
Lady Macbeth realizes the permanence of her actions after the murder of King Duncan.
“Fair is foul, and foul is fair.”
Macbeth1606
Spoken by the witches, establishing a world where moral boundaries are blurred.
“Uneasy lies the head that wears a crown.”
Henry IV1597
King Henry reflects on the burdens of leadership and power.
“All that glitters is not gold.”
Merchant of Venice1596
The line warns against judging based on appearance alone.
“The lady doth protest too much.”
Hamlet1603
Queen Gertrude comments on exaggerated expressions of innocence.
