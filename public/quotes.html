Shakespeare’s Timeless Words

The enduring value of works written by William Shakespeare is his ability to express the complexity of the human emotional experience. His characters grapple with many different aspects of the human condition: life, love, ambition, betrayal, self-identity and destiny - themes that today's audiences continue to relate to. The language in Shakespeare's works is always precise, vivid and evocative; some passages are so powerful and well constructed that a reader often feels as though they have walked through an entire world when they have only read several short lines of text.

Through these quotes, we see the voice of the author, a philosophy, romance, humour and tragic events which ultimately reinforce his humanity. These quotes also allow us to understand how every generation has experienced problems, hope and dream of something larger than themselves. The quotes also show that the impact of art continues beyond the Shakespeare’s life and affects people hundreds of years after he has died.
