"All the world’s a stage…" - William Shakespeare

Shakespeare’s Life & Legacy

Early Life

Born in Stratford-upon-Avon, 1564. Grew up in a modest family, showing early interest in storytelling and poetry.

William Shakespeare was baptized on April 26, 1564. His father was a glove-maker and wool merchant.

London Years

Moved to London in the late 1580s, where he began his career as an actor and playwright, gaining fame with his early works.

Shakespeare's plays were performed at The Globe Theatre. He became a prominent figure in Elizabethan theatre.

Later Works

Produced some of his most famous plays and sonnets, including Hamlet, Othello, and King Lear.

His later works reflect deep insights into human nature, tragedy, and comedy, cementing his legacy.

Legacy

William Shakespeare's influence endures in literature, theatre, and culture worldwide.

His works are studied and performed globally, inspiring countless adaptations and reinterpretations.
